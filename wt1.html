Slalom Consulting

Slalom is a large consulting company that is present in numerous industries. It was first established in 2001, and now has over 14000 employees. Some of Slalom's primary industries are Technology, Healthcare, and Financial Services. Canada is one of eight of Slalom's primary locations. Slalom approaches consulting by striving to be as human as possible to give their clients the best experience they can give.

Employer

As mentioned, Slalom makes an effort to be as human as possible when working with clients. Not only with clients, but with each other as well. The people at Slalom consistently push their 10 core values and follow them each and every day. Throughout my time here, I was never afraid of asking "dumb" questions and was always encouraged to speak up if I wasn't understanding something. Slalom curates a very learning-oriented work model, always encouraging its employees to be curious and try new things.

Slalom is also closely partnered with some of the biggest technology companies of the world today, including Microsoft, AWS, Google, and Salesforce. This contributes to Slalom's technological branch significantly. Salesforce and Microsoft products are used on a day-to-day basis and are central to the business operations and communication within the company. Slalom consistently strives for innovation in the technology space. They have dove head first into creating AI products to aid with internal tasks, as well as with client solutions. Slalom also creates many unique, in-house applications to be able to have the tools that are tailored specifically to their needs.

Job Roles - Business Operations Intern
Data Analytics

My primary role throughout my internship at Slalom was working closley with their company-wide data. As part of this, I got to learn Salesforce and Power BI at a deep level, becoming very profficient at them by the end.

In Salesforce, I made numerous reports and dashboards ranging from financial data to information about campaigns, opportunities, and individual clients. Salesforce is rooted in Slalom's day to day operations, and as such, was in mine as well. I also used Salesforce to be able to track how various things such as client engagements and campaigns are doing.

In Power BI, I worked closely with one of the leaders of Slalom's Montreal market. We had a near daily back and forth throughout the creation process and refinement of a Power BI dashboard. This dashboard consisted of numerous different visuals, such as tables, matrices, bar graphs, line graphs, and circle graphs, overviewing the entire business' earned and forecasted revenue, as well as the revenue goals. Upon completion, this dashboard got shared amongst numerous Slalom coworkers, serving as a useful visual tool for decision-making and understanding the current state of the business. Working on this dashboard, I learned a lot about the intricicies of Power BI, including managing inter-table relationships, creating custom measures, and linking multiple datapoints together. Not only that, but working on this dashboard truly felt impactful for the company. The dashboard I made may very well become one of the company's core dashboards used by a lot of people.

R4 Event

R4 is Slalom's largest annual event that they hold. It is an event that intends to bring together the leaders of all of Slalom's clients in hopes of building relationships and new connections amongst each other. Each year also gets their set of guest speakers from a wide variety of industries, such as sports, movies, and other public figures. Their most well-known speaker was Michelle Obama last year! The speaker list for this year is featured below.

I worked pretty closely with the invite process for the R4 event. Many leaders across the country were responsible for identifying various contacts that they wanted to invite to the event, and I shared some repsponsibility in ensuring data correctness of these contacts. Salesforce data of these contacts gets pulled into automated email invites once a week. Each time those data pulls came around, I was tasked with reaching out to each contact's related Slalom leader to ensure data correctness. I was also able to answer many questions these leaders had about the invite process was. It was great being able to work so closely with them, and I greatly improved my communication skills in doing so!

Exception Reporting

The last major ongoing responsibility I had during my internship at Slalom was Exception Reporting for the company's opportunities entered in Salesforce. Opportunities are Slalom's potential projects and contracts with clients. Exception reporting consists of identifying various data-related problems with these opportunities. Some examples of problems were dates that were no longer the actual target date, staffing plans that were not fully updates, or some opportunities just no longer being relevant. It was my job to go in and investigate any opportunities that were flagged with potential problems and communicate these problems to the opportunities' corresponding coordinator. Doing so helped me a lot with my communication skills since I reached out about these opportunity exceptions a few times every week. I had to articulate the problem to my coworkers who may not have been as well versed in Salesforce, as well as suggest logical solutions to these exceptions.

Goals
Goal #1 - Personal Organization and Time Management

My first goal had to do with Personal Organization and Time Management:
My goal was to develop my skills in self-scheduling my various tasks within a professional environment, consisting of tasks of varying priority, as well as group tasks.

While I already had good time management skills in a school setting, learning to schedule each day in a work environment proved to be a different experience. There were numerous times at Slalom where I had multiple tasks on the go at once. Because of this, I was able to work on integrating task prioritization into my time management skill set. The factors I analyzed to be able to prioritize my tasks were how quick the task needed to be completed, how long it would likely take to complete, and if I had any other meetings/commitments in the coming days. By putting all of this together, I developed a solid strategy to ensure I complete all my tasks on time.

This strategy was put to the test during one of the last weeks of my internship where my supervisor took a week-long vacation. That meant that a lot of their work would fall onto my shoulders, including others coming to me asking for technological guidance as well as having to reach out to my coworkers regarding invites for the R4 event. There came a point in the week where I had about 5 different things going on at once, which did become overwhelming at first. But, because of the organizational skills I picked up throughout my work term, I was able to rise to the challenge and successfully complete every task that came my way. This was a defining moment that proved to me that I had achieved my goal in further developing my time-management skills and my ability to apply them in a work setting.

Goal #2 - Technological Literacy

My next goal was all about Technological Literacy:
The goal was to improve my knowledge, understanding, and proficiency in new and familiar modern-day technologies in hopes of being brought with me into my future career endeavours.

Going into this internship, which was very data-oriented, the only database technology I was familiar with was SQL. Right away, on my first day of work, I was introduced to Slalom’s primary data tools that were used on a daily basis; Salesforce and Power BI. I had barely heard of either of these, but was very eager to learn them, and my supervisor was very eager to teach me as well! This made for a very enjoyable work environment where I was never afraid to ask questions.

I began working primarily in Salesforce, learning about its major attributes, including Reports, Campaigns, Dashboards, and Engagements. Salesforce is very central to Slalom’s day-to-day work with their clients, which enabled me to learn how to create reports from multiple sources, as well as transforming them into visually appealing dashboards. From starting off not even hearing about Salesforce, I became pretty proficient and able to navigate my way around. So much so that I was actually able to obtain a certification as a Salesforce Associate thanks to all that I learned.

Additionally, after some time into my internship, I started a pretty big project for one of Slalom’s leaders in Montreal. I was tasked with sprucing up a financial Excel file of his to go from being exclusively for Montreal to being for all of Canada. It was here that I learned a lot of new aspects of Excel that I had never worked with before, including Pivot Tables, conditional formatting, and using External Data Connections. This expanded my knowledge of the possibilities of what Excel could be used for. Pivot Tables can allow for Excel documents to be a lot more dynamic, interactive, and customizable. And creating External Data Connections to Salesforce and Power BI increases the reporting potential of Excel and reduces a lot of manual data entry.

Finally, after translating the Montreal file to work for all of Canada, I was then tasked to translate that Excel file into Power BI to make it much more visually appealing and comprehensive. This provided me with a vast amount of time to play around and learn a lot of the intricacies of Power BI. I learned a lot about creating custom measures, creating Foreign Key relationships between tables, combining multiple datasets together, and creating concise visuals. After working on the Power BI dashboard as my primary ongoing project of my internship, I became extremely proficient and comfortable in Power BI.

In conclusion, I would say that I met this goal in a big way. Not only did I learn new features of Excel, a tool that I was already familiar with, but I was also introduced to and became very proficient in two brand new technologies, being Salesforce and Power BI. I will definitely be able to use the knowledge that I have gained in my future career endeavours.

Goal #3 - Inquiry & Analysis

My third and final goal of this work term had to do with Inquiry & Analysis: My goal was to learn and practice my ability to listen to both coworkers as well as external clients and pose questions to ensure I understand what they are asking. I want to not be afraid of how my questions may be perceived upon asking them. If I do not understand something and need further clarification, I strive to be able to simply ask the question and seek the guidance I need. However, at the same time, I want to ensure I do my best to gather any information I can about the topic prior to inquiring about it, so that I only pose meaningful questions instead of ones where the answer can be found easily.

While I definitely think I made some progress on this goal throughout my work term, I still have work to do. Asking questions when I wasn’t understanding something was constantly in my thoughts during every meeting I was a part of. I made sure to go outside my comfort zone and pose meaningful questions as I felt were needed. I also made a strong effort to take notes during my conversations with my coworkers to help retain and further understand what is being asked of me. Even still, despite my best efforts, there were times where I shied away from asking questions out of the worry of appearing inadequate. This occasionally caused a slowdown of progress on my tasks, as I had to spend a while trying to figure out what exactly I was supposed to do.

Overall, Slalom’s growth-oriented environment made for a great place to help me develop this goal. I was often encouraged by my supervisor as well as my other coworkers to speak up if I wasn’t following, which helped to make me feel more comfortable to do so. It was also helpful to learn from their example and see them pose meaningful questions without a second thought. The same goes for note-taking as well. My supervisor constantly makes notes during meetings to help herself stay on top of everything that’s asked of her. Seeing this strategy in action inspired me to do the same. Although I was far from perfect when it came to these things, not all goals can be expected to be achieved in one work term, and I still made a lot of important steps in the right direction even if I’m not fully there yet.

Conclusion

Overall, my time at Slalom Consulting was incredibly valuble as my first Co-op Work Term. With an exceptionally kind-hearted and growth-oriented work environment, I was given numerous opportunities to grow and progress myself in my career. Through working closely with company-wide data anlytics, I became proficient in two brand-new technologies. Additionally, when ensuring data-cleanliness for Slalom's R4 event, as well as their many consulting opportunities, I got to work on and improve my communication skills by reaching out to coworkers on the daily. My first work term at Slalom was very growth-oriented and I very much look forward to continuing that growth in my future work terms.
